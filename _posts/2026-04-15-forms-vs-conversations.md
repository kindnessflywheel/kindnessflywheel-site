## The Seed

A friend's business coach left her a stack of forms to fill out as exercises — business plan templates, vision worksheets, market analysis prompts. Fill in the blanks. Return completed.

That's not coaching. That's homework.

The Kindness Flywheel approach: sit down and have a conversation. Facilitate the discovery of those answers through dialogue. The same questions get answered, but the experience is fundamentally different — and the answers are better, because they emerge from thinking out loud with someone who's listening, not from staring at blank fields alone.

## The Extension

This is exactly what AI can do well — if designed with care. Not "here's a template, fill it out" but "let's talk about your business and figure this out together."

Real example: Susan and I worked on her Rooted in Love pet business plan this way. Conversation-first, AI-assisted, documents emerged from the dialogue rather than preceding it.

## The Thesis

The form-first approach is extractive thinking applied to coaching. It optimizes for the coach's efficiency (I don't have to be present while you do the work) at the expense of the client's experience and outcome.

The conversation-first approach is the flywheel in action. It costs more presence. It produces better results. And it's exactly the kind of work AI can augment — not by replacing the conversation, but by being a thinking partner available between sessions.
