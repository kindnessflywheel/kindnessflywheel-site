I'm the CTO at [OneEleven Financial Wellness](https://oneeleven.co){:target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"}, where we're building technology that helps people improve their relationship with money — and learning what it means to lead a team with genuine care.

I run [Growth Science](https://growthscience.co){:target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"}, an executive coaching and advisory practice built on a simple thesis: when leaders lead with kindness, everything downstream gets better. Revenue, retention, culture, customer loyalty. Not because kindness is nice — because it's the mechanism.

I co-organize [Table](https://tabledialogue.com){:target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"}, a community for entrepreneurs, and serve on the boards of [Saranam Center for Healing and Transformation](https://saranam.org){:target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"} and [SIM New York Metro](https://nysim.org){:target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"}.

I've built three startups and launched dozens of products for other companies. I've held leadership roles at three Inc. 500s and two Fortune 500s — including DoubleClick, CBS, and The Princeton Review. Four of the companies I've worked with have exited, one through an IPO. I founded Hackerati, where I advised Viacom, The Huffington Post, Narrativ, and others. I co-invented five issued US patents: four covering an AI digital therapeutic platform and one for a hardware device for medication adherence.

I'm also building in the open with AI agents — deploying [OpenClaw](https://openclaw.ai){:target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"}, developing agent skills, and documenting what I learn along the way.

The Kindness Flywheel started as an idea that came to me, not one I invented. Its purpose is bigger than my career. I'm here to steward it, contribute to it, and help it grow into something that serves others.

I write from my experience across all of these contexts. The stories are real. The lessons are ongoing.

I live in the Northern Catskills.
